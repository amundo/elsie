In documentary linguistics, there is a principle that says "never
erase anything."

The reason for this principle is that the process of writing down
an unwritten lagnuage is inherently inaccurate, and linguists tends
to vacillate in their transcriptions, as they struggle to figure
out the sound system and so on.

Practically speaking, this means that in their field notebooks
linguists a) write in ink and b) only indicate a correction by
striking through old text, in such a way that it remains legible.
